Leading Causes of Death and Their Relationship to Mental Health

A comparative analysis of mortality patterns in the United States and Mexico. Explore how major causes of death relate to mental health trends over time and across regions.

Explore the Data

Project Overview

This website compares the top 9 leading causes of death with mental health, highlighting differences between the United States and Mexico. Use the dropdowns to explore bar charts, trend lines, state-level comparisons, and a mental-health relationship view.

This project was created to better understand how physical health outcomes and mental health trends intersect across different national contexts. By comparing the United States and Mexico, we aim to highlight how social, economic, and healthcare systems may influence both leading causes of death and mental health–related mortality. Visualizing these patterns together allows for a more nuanced interpretation of public health challenges and encourages critical reflection on prevention, policy, and access to care. Due to the reason accident with Brown we decided to do a compare analyis on how deaths can impact mental health in both countries.
